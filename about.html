A little more about me

Designer, researcher,
perpetual learner.

Product designer + UX researcher driven by curiosity, inclusion, and the belief that thoughtful design can make the world more human.

I'm Jasmine Sayed — a Product Designer and UX Researcher pursuing my Master's in Human Centered Design & Engineering at the University of Washington ('27).

I've worked with Microsoft, Amazon, and a handful of startups building research-driven, human-centered experiences — especially at the crossroads of AI and accessibility.

I do both design and research because I've seen what happens when they're siloed. The best work starts with understanding people deeply and ends with designs that reflect what you learned — closing the gap between insight and interface.

📍 San Francisco, CA & Seattle, WA SF native, currently in Seattle — city girl at heart!

How I Work

01

Research before pixels

I don't start designing until I understand the people I'm designing for. Interviews, observations, and data come first.

02

Design for the edges

If it works for the most constrained user, it works for everyone. Accessibility and inclusion are the starting point, not an afterthought.

03

Show the thinking

Good design looks obvious in hindsight. I make sure stakeholders see the reasoning behind every decision.
