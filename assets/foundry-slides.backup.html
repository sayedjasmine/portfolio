03

The challenge

Foundry was launching to developers, but clicks weren't going far.

Developers would land, poke at agent creation, and bounce. Where and why was on me to find out.

What we needed to learn
  • A developer's mental model when adding data to an agent
  • Where the interface pulls attention first
  • When confusion becomes a hard blocker
How I tested it

Heuristic eval + Think Aloud against one scenario: "Help a baker optimize their recipe site with an AI agent."

Conducted 8 moderated sessions · Dovetail tagging · Nielsen 0–4 severity.

07

Key design decisions

Three forks in the road: what I shipped.

Friction · Hard to discover
01
Search-first or filter-first?
Considered A prominent search bar (Hugging Face approach)
Chose Task tiles → filter sidebar with region & capability promoted
Card sort: devs rank region + capability above model names. They know what to build, not which model.
Friction · Region revealed too late
02
Where does region live?
Considered Region only in advanced filters · region revealed at deploy
Chose Region in the row + as primary filter + in the deploy drawer
100% of usability friction was region-related. Couldn't risk burying it again.
Friction · Hard to compare
03
Comparison: separate page or in flow?
Considered Dedicated compare page (Vertex AI) · modal popup
Chose Persistent compare tray pinned to the Models grid
Separate pages kill context. The tray keeps comparison in the decision moment.

Inspired by competitive analysis of Hugging Face · Google Vertex AI · Amazon Bedrock · Optimizely Opal.
